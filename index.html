I hold a bachelor degree in Graphic Design & Multimedia, which laid the foundation for my obsession with visual systems, typography, and motion. I specialize in transforming complex problems into elegant, intuitive experiences — from initial concept through final pixel to production-ready animation.

My work sits at the intersection of aesthetics and functionality. I've collaborated with startups, agencies, and global brands to craft products that are both beautiful and meaningful.

Vault Finance —
Trust through Simplicity

The Problem

Vault's onboarding converted just 22% of signups. Users hit a 14-step KYC flow and abandoned — trust never established, complexity never justified.

The Approach

Resequenced onboarding to lead with value — show the product before asking for data. Chunked KYC into 3 digestible stages with transparent progress. Added micro-animations to signal security without jargon.

HealthPulse —
Making Medical UX Human

The Problem

62% of patients abandoned the appointment booking flow. Confusing navigation, medical jargon, and a form-heavy design made a stressful experience worse — especially for older users.

The Approach

Moderated usability testing with 18 participants across age groups. Rebuilt the flow around plain language, larger touch targets, and a symptom-first navigation model. Every screen tested twice before dev handoff.

Ember Shop —
Conversion by Design

The Problem

Ember had strong traffic but a 1.8% conversion rate. Cart abandonment at checkout hit 78% — customers were slipping away at the last step due to friction, distrust signals, and a 6-step checkout.

The Approach

Funnel analysis + session recordings identified the exact drop-off moments. Redesigned checkout as a single-page flow, added trust signals at decision points, optimized product pages for mobile-first browsing patterns.

Every project starts with a discovery phase — user interviews, competitor analysis, and stakeholder workshops. I don't open Figma until we've agreed on the problem we're solving. From there: information architecture → wireframes → high-fidelity UI → interactive prototype → handoff. You're involved at every stage, not just the reveal.

A focused landing page or marketing site takes 1–2 weeks. A full product — web app or mobile app with multiple flows — typically runs 4–8 weeks depending on scope and how quickly feedback rounds move. I'll give you a detailed timeline before we start, and I stick to it.

Both — and I genuinely enjoy the constraint of a pre-seed budget. For early-stage startups I often recommend a focused MVP design: one user flow, done exceptionally well, that validates your core value proposition. You don't need 50 screens before you talk to users. You need 5 that prove the concept.

A brief describing your goals, audience, and any existing brand assets (logo, colors, fonts if you have them). That's it to get started. The clearer the brief, the faster we move — but I can also help you define the brief if you're not sure where to begin. We'll cover everything in the kickoff call.

Yes — developer collaboration is built into every project. I deliver Figma files with component annotations, spacing specs, responsive behavior documentation, and exported assets. I'm also available for developer Q&A during build, and I review the live implementation before launch to catch anything that drifted from the design.

Every project includes post-launch support (1–30 days depending on tier). For ongoing needs, the monthly retainer is the most efficient option — it gives you a dedicated design partner without the overhead of hiring in-house. Many clients move to a retainer after their first project.
